\section{SODA-QFT: Semantic Dynamics and Multi-Field Quantum Evolution}
\label{sec:soda_qft}

The SODA framework is extended to integrate Quantum Field Theory (QFT) and Semantic Information Theory, resulting in the \textbf{SODA-QFT engine}: a multi-field, semantically grounded, GPU-accelerated evolutionary system. Each Nucleus no longer carries a single complex state $\psi_j$, but a \textit{multi-field spinor} (vector of complex wavefunctions):

\[
\Psi_j = 
\begin{bmatrix}
\psi^{(1)}_j \\
\psi^{(2)}_j \\
\vdots \\
\psi^{(F)}_j
\end{bmatrix}
\in \mathbb{C}^F,
\]

where each component corresponds to a structural modality (Chaos, Bayesian, SNN, etc.), enabling multi-perspective reasoning and cross-field information transfer. The dimensionality $F$ is typically small ($F \le 8$).

\subsection{Multi-Field Fused Quantum Kuramoto Map (QKM)}
\label{sec:qkm_multi}

The QKM is generalized to account for cross-field interactions. The update rule for the target field $a$ of Nucleus $j$ is defined by the total coupling sum $S_j^{(a)}$, which integrates contributions from all source fields $b$ across all Nuclei $k$:

\[
\Psi_j(t+1) 
= 
\frac{1}{Z_j}
\left[
\Psi_j(t) 
+ \sum_{k=1}^{N}
\sum_{b=1}^{F}
J_{jk}^{(a,b)} 
\, e^{i(\phi_k^{(b)} - \phi_j^{(a)})}
\, \psi_k^{(b)}(t)
+ \Delta \omega_j \Psi_j(t)
\right].
\]

The multi-field coupling tensor $J_{jk}^{(a,b)}$ encodes three critical interaction effects:

\[
J_{jk}^{(a,b)} = 
\underbrace{\exp\left( -\frac{|f_j^{(a)} - f_k^{(b)}|}{T} \right)}_{\text{Fitness Proximity}}
\cdot 
\underbrace{\exp\left( -|H_j^{(a)} - H_k^{(b)}| \right)}_{\text{Entropy Polarity}}
\cdot 
\underbrace{\Lambda_{ab}}_{\text{Semantic Interaction}}.
\]

The interaction is minimized for high error/dissimilarity (Fitness Proximity) and maximized by high divergence (Entropy Polarity) and high semantic compatibility ($\Lambda_{ab}$).

\subsection{Semantic Interaction Matrix $\Lambda$ (Meaning as a Field)}
\label{sec:semantic_lambda}

The **Semantic Interaction Matrix $\mathbf{\Lambda}$** replaces the static coupling factor $G$. It measures the semantic compatibility $\lambda_{ab}$ between field $a$ and field $b$, derived from their information geometry (embeddings $\mathbf{e}_a$):

\[
\Lambda = 
\begin{bmatrix}
1 & \lambda_{12} & \cdots & \lambda_{1F} \\
\lambda_{21} & 1 & \cdots & \lambda_{2F} \\
\vdots & \vdots & \ddots & \vdots \\
\lambda_{F1} & \lambda_{F2} & \cdots & 1
\end{bmatrix}.
\]

We adopt the softmax-normalized cosine similarity (based on per-field embeddings $\mathbf{e}$) to dynamically compute $\lambda_{ab}$:

\[
\lambda_{ab} 
= 
\frac{
\exp\left( \frac{\cos(\mathbf{e}_a, \mathbf{e}_b)}{\tau} \right)
}{
\sum_{c=1}^{F} 
\exp\left( \frac{\cos(\mathbf{e}_a, \mathbf{e}_c)}{\tau} \right)
}.
\]

This mechanism establishes that **meaning is modeled as an interaction field**, shaping how distributed computational entities interfere, synchronize, and evolve.

\subsection{Quantum Field Evolution and GPU Acceleration}

The structural dynamics (Fusion and Decomposition) operate directly on the spinor field $\Psi$.

\subsubsection{Structural Evolution via Spinor Fields}

\begin{enumerate}
    \item \textbf{Field Fusion}: When the system is highly synchronized ($|\mathcal{R}| \to 1$), high-entropy Nuclei ($\mathcal{C}$) fuse into a higher-order **"field condensate"** $\Psi_{\text{fused}}$ (analogous to bound states):
    \[
    \Psi_{\text{fused}} = 
    \frac{
    \sum_{j \in \mathcal{C}} H_j \Psi_j
    }{
    \sum_{j \in \mathcal{C}} H_j
    }.
    \]
    \item \textbf{Semantic Decomposition}: Low-fitness Nuclei are split into offspring, where the perturbation $\epsilon$ is drawn from the semantic geometry, forming the world’s first \textbf{Semantic Quantum Field Evolution Mechanism}:
    \[
    \Psi_{j,\text{child}} 
    = 
    \Psi_j + \epsilon \cdot \mathcal{N}(0, \mathbf{\Lambda}).
    \]
\end{enumerate}

\subsubsection{HPC Fused Kernel Implementation}

To manage the $O(N^2 \cdot F^2)$ complexity of multi-field QKM, the core dynamics are implemented via a custom **CUDA Fused Kernel**:

\begin{itemize}
    \item The kernel eliminates large intermediate tensors for $J_{jk}$ and phase matrices.
    \item It utilizes **Shared Memory Tiling** and **Register Accumulation** to execute the $N \times N \times F \times F$ cross-field interactions.
    \item This design minimizes global memory traffic, achieving a validated $\mathbf{12\times}$–$\mathbf{24\times}$ speedup over PyTorch broadcasting (See Section~\ref{sec:benchmark}).
\end{itemize}
